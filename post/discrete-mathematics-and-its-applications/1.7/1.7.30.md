Prove that $$m^2 = n^2$$ if and only if m = n or m = -n.

Solution

+ p: $$m^2 = n^2$$
+ q: $$(m = n) \vee (m = -n)$$

**Proof $$p \rightarrow q$$**

Assume q is false

$$m \ne n, m^2 \ne n^2$$

$$m \ne -n, m^2 \ne (-n)^2 = n^2$$

p is false.

**Proof $$q \rightarrow p$$**

m = n, $$m^2 = n^2$$

m = -n, $$m^2 = (-n)^2 = n^2$$
